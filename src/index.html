Our Comprehensive Initiative To Solve Those Problems

  • Provide A Cooperative Workspace

    1 in 68 children (1 in 42 boys and 1 in 189 girls) is diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). 

    In Arizona the incidence is 1 in 64. This is 1.5 million individuals with autism according to a US News and World Report 2015. We Plan to Address the problem of the steady growth of autism and DD with sustainable programs, that emphasize “outdoor and outside activity,” to develop happy, skilled, social, contributing members of the community.

  • Advocate For Recreational Therapy (RT)

    RT is grossly under-represented in the many therapies prescribed for those with developmental disabilities. One of the many benefits of RT is the interaction with one’s community. RT helps those individuals navigate available resources to enhance their overall quality of life.

  • Assist with Career Opportunities

    35 percent of young adults (ages 19-23) with autism have not had a job or received postgraduate education after leaving high school. (Shattuck et al., 2012) Also, 78 percent of adults with developmental disabilities are unemployed. (Butterworth et al. 2010)

    We Plan To Assist in providing transitional training and employment opportunities including outdoor-oriented job programs such as horse and pet boarding, and gardening.

Take the stand for Individualized Education and Programs (IEP)

 In keeping with todays new work paradigm, we strive to support the various professionals that work toward the success of these individuals. This necessitates the need for cooperative workspaces to educate, instruct and provide small group social interaction.

  • our mission
    a commitment to help those affected by developmental disablities

    As a non-profit public charity, we endeavor to enrich the lives of individuals and the families of those affected by autism and other developmental disabilities (DD) by promoting an active, independent lifestyle with an emphasis on “outdoor and outside” activity.

  • our goals
    No one should be confined to home with nothing to do.

    To assist in providing an active lifestyle for those with (DD) by supporting companies, partnerships, events, and programs that help these individuals be challenged; attain goals; and live an enriched life. 

  • our vision
    To ultimately create sustainable multidimensional ranch-like communities

    We want to create these (“RB Ranches”) that support and house organizations and programs that provide educational, therapeutic, transitional training, and vocational/employment programs for those with DD in a safe, social, and encouraging environment.

Donor Privacy Policy

We will not sell, share or trade our donors' names or personal information with any other entity, nor send mailings to our donors on behalf of other organizations.

This policy applies to all information received by Reaching Branch Foundation, both online and offline, on any Platform, as well as any electronic, written, or oral communications.

To the extent any donations are processed through a third-party service provider, our donors’ information will only be used for purposes necessary to process the donation.
