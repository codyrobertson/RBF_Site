As a branch grows, it actively reaches out in many directions to find its way and to add support for strength. 

The Foundation was started by parents who value the individuals and programs that enable those with autism and other developmental disabilities to grow stronger and actively reach beyond their perceived limits.

We believe goals are achieved with the help of a support system that enhances their strength and endurance for physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being.

  • our mission
    a commitment to help those affected by developmental disablities

    As a non-profit public charity, we endeavor to enrich the lives of individuals and the families of those affected by autism and other developmental disabilities (DD) by promoting an active, independent lifestyle with an emphasis on “outdoor and outside” activity.

  • our goals
    No one should be confined to home with nothing to do.

    To assist in providing an active lifestyle for those with (DD) by supporting companies, partnerships, events, and programs that help these individuals be challenged; attain goals; and live an enriched life. 

  • our vision
    To ultimately create sustainable multidimensional ranch-like communities

    We want to create these (“RB Ranches”) that support and house organizations and programs that provide educational, therapeutic, transitional training, and vocational/employment programs for those with DD in a safe, social, and encouraging environment.

The RBF Solution: A Comprehensive Initiative

  • Address the problem at it's root cause

    Address the problem of the steady growth of autism and DD with sustainable programs, that emphasize “outdoor and outside activity,” to develop happy, skilled, social, contributing members of the community.

  • Advocate For Recreational Therapy (RT)

    RT is grossly under-represented in the many therapies prescribed for those with developmental disabilities. One of the many benefits of RT is the interaction with one’s community. RT helps those individuals navigate available resources to enhance their overall quality of life.  

  • Foster Development

    Encourage the development of special-needs playgrounds and parks. Research shows being in a natural setting awakens opioid receptors, which not only inhibit pain but also improve emotional bonds and positive thinking. (Shelhub and Logan, 2016)

  • Assist with  Career Opportunities

    Assist in providing transitional training and employment opportunities including outdoor-oriented job programs such as horse and pet boarding, and gardening. 

  • Fuel Research into Residential Support Option

    Research and plan residential support and innovative housing options for the growing demands of special-needs housing.
