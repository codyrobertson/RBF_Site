Enabling the developmentally disabled to live a normal life.

What dirves us

As a branch grows,
it actively reaches out in many directions to find
its way and to add support for strength.

Individuals with autism and other developmental disabilities grow stronger
through actively reaching beyond their perceived limits. Goals are reached
with the help of a support system that enhances their strength and
endurance for physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being.

Our Mission

As a non-profit public charity, we endeavor to enrich the lives of individuals and the
families of those affected by autism and other developmental disabilities through
supporting companies, parnerships, or events that promote an active, independent
lifestyle with an emphasis on outdoor and outside activity.

Our Goal

We are driven to provide the support that emphasizes an active lifestyle for those with developmental disability (DD) and to assist in the development of places and programs for individuals to be challenged, attain goals, and live an enriched life. (No one should be confined to home with nothing to do!)

Our Vision

To build, operate, and facilitate sustainable multidimensional ranch-like communities (“RB Ranches”) that support and house organizations and programs that provide educational, transitional training, and vocational / employment programs for those with DD in a safe, social, and encouraging environment.

How We Plan To Do This

A Rising Need For Support

There are very few comprehensive programs for lower functioning individuals.
Growing number of childhood diagnoses

1 in 68 children (1 in 42 boys and 1 in 189 girls) are being diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). In Arizona the incidence is 1 in 64. This is 1.5 million individuals with autism according to a US News and World Report 2015.

Creating more young adult un-employment

35 percent of young adults (ages 19-23) with autism have not had a job or received postgraduate education after leaving high school. (Shattuck et al., 2012) Also, 78 percent of adults with developmental disabilities are unemployed. (Butterworth et al. 2010)

lifetime care

$3.2 million needed to support each throughout their lifetimes. (Ganz 2007)

How We Will Solve This: The 5 A's

  • Address

    Address the problem of the steady growth of autism and DD with sustainable
    programs, that emphasize outdoor and outside activity, to develop happy, skilled,
    social, contributing members of the community.

  • Advocate

    Advocate for Recreational Therapy (RT). RT is grossly under-represented in the
    many therapies prescribed for those with developmental disabilities. In this ranch
    setting, RT is given in a fun outdoor experience that goes beyond any one individual
    therapy.

  • Accomplish

    Provide transitional training and employment opportunities. Outdoor oriented
    programs of this type are all but non-existent.

  • Accommodate

    Research and plan innovative housing options for the growing demands of special
    needs housing.

  • Amplify 

    A special needs playground/park would be an exceptional enhancement to the area
    for locals and visitors as well. Research shows being in a natural setting awakens opioid
    receptors, which not only inhibit pain but also improve emotional bonds and positive
    thinking. (Shelhub and Logan, 2016)
